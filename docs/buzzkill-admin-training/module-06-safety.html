Core Rule Do not guess. Do not answer from memory. Refer, verify, and escalate.
In Practice
Safety questions are different from general scheduling questions. Customers may ask whether a product is safe for pets, whether children need to leave, or whether food surfaces need preparation. These questions should not be answered casually. Your job is to route the question to the SDS, product label, technician, or manager — and document that you did so.

What Is an SDS?

Safety Data Sheet (SDS)
OSHA Hazard Communication Standard
1 Identification
2 Hazard Identification
3 Composition / Ingredients
4 First-Aid Measures
5 Firefighting Measures
6 Accidental Release
7 Handling & Storage
8 Exposure Controls / PPE
9–16 Physical properties, toxicology, transport…
Per OSHA: SDSs provide information on chemical properties, hazards, protective measures, and precautions for handling, storage, and transport.
📄
What it is for admin
When a customer asks a product-specific safety question, the SDS is the authoritative reference — not your memory, not what you've heard before. Do not improvise safety guidance.
🗂️
Where SDSs are kept
Know where to find BuzzKill's SDS documents for products in use. Your trainer will walk you through the company's SDS access process.
🔁
Your role
You are not expected to read and interpret SDS documents for customers. Your role is to route the question to the person who can — the technician or manager — and document that you did so.
In Practice
An SDS is a formal safety reference for a chemical product. For admin support, the important takeaway is simple: when the question is product-specific, do not rely on memory. Route the question, reference the SDS, and document the interaction.
Section 3 of 5

What Is a Product Label?

Pesticide Product Label
EPA Registration No. xxxxxxx
Directions for Use
It is a violation of Federal law to use this product in a manner inconsistent with its labeling.
Precautionary Statements
Keep out of reach of children. Harmful if swallowed. Avoid contact with skin, eyes, and clothing.
Re-entry Interval
Do not enter or allow others to enter treated areas until sprays have dried.
Environmental Hazards
This product is toxic to fish and aquatic invertebrates. Do not apply near water.
Use Sites / Application Limits
For use in residential structures. Apply to cracks and crevices only.
Per EPA: Pesticide labels provide critical information about how to safely and legally use pesticide products. Pesticide labels are legally enforceable.
⚖️
Labels are legally enforceable
This is why BuzzKill does not rely on casual explanations. The label is the legal document — what it says is what governs use.
📋
Labels answer re-entry questions
When customers ask "when can I go back in?", the product label — not admin memory — provides the authoritative answer for that specific product.
🐾
Labels address pets and surfaces
Pet safety, food surfaces, and specific use restrictions are all addressed in the label. Route the question; don't guess.
🔁
Different products, different rules
A blanket answer about safety doesn't work. Each product has its own label. The technician assigned to the service knows which product applies.
In Practice
The product label is a legal document. This is why BuzzKill should not rely on casual explanations when customers ask product-specific questions. The label matters — and so does your documentation of how you handled the question.
Section 4 of 5

Questions Admin Should Not Answer From Memory

✗"Is this safe for my baby?"
✗"Can my dog lick the floor after treatment?"
✗"Do I need to leave the house?"
✗"What exact chemical will be used?"
✗"Can this be used around food?"
✗"Is it safe if I have asthma?"
✗"How long until I can touch treated areas?"
✗"What happens if someone was exposed?"
The Correct Response to All of These
I'm going to route that to the proper safety reference and technician or manager. I want to make sure you get accurate information, not my best guess.
In Practice
These questions may sound simple, but they carry real safety implications. Do not say something is "completely safe." Do not give medical advice. Do not answer re-entry or contact questions unless the answer has been verified through the correct source. Document the question and that you routed it — every time.
Section 5 of 5

Approved Safety Language

Use these phrases:
"For product-specific safety information, we refer to the SDS and product label."
"A technician can confirm the treatment details for your specific service."
"I do not want to give you an inaccurate safety answer."
"I'll document your question and route it to the appropriate person."
"Please follow the instructions provided by the technician and any written preparation materials."
Avoid these phrases:
"It's harmless."
"You'll definitely be fine."
"No precautions are needed."
"I think the product is safe."
"Don't worry about it."
Why This Builds Trust
Saying "I do not want to give you inaccurate information" is not weakness — it is professional accuracy. Customers appreciate honesty paired with action. It shows that BuzzKill takes safety seriously and that you are the kind of person who gets things right rather than just moving on.
Never Do This Do not answer a safety question and then fail to document it. Even if you route it correctly, the interaction should be in the record.
