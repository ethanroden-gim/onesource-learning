Rule The task is not complete until the correct system is updated and the next action is clear. Answered ≠ done.
In Practice
Most admin tasks follow the same basic pattern. First, understand who is contacting us and what they need. Then verify the customer, property, and service history. Determine the next step — answer, schedule, update, assign, or escalate. The task is not complete until the correct system is updated and the next action is clear.

For condo and HOA properties, capture the community name, unit or common area, and authorization context.

Access Notes — Never Skip These
Gate codes or locked entry points
Lockbox location and combination
Unit access — self, management, or resident must be present
Pets — type, containment, or removal needed
Parking restrictions or instructions
Tenant coordination or notification required
Common area vs. unit — or both
Before Finalizing — Ask Yourself
"If I were the technician, would I know where to go, what the issue is, how to access the area, and who to contact?"
In Practice
Scheduling is more than finding an open time slot. The technician needs enough information to arrive prepared. Always confirm the correct account and location. If the appointment involves an association, confirm whether the technician needs access to a common area, a specific unit, or multiple areas.

Estimates & Service Agreements

Admin Handles
Start estimate request and gather property/pest details
Confirm customer contact information is complete
Send approved documents from the correct source
Track signature or approval status
Update account status in FieldRoutes
Follow up on outstanding documents
Route to Manager / Technician
Pricing and scope decisions
Warranty terms or treatment promises
Service agreement review and final approval
Custom or complex quote requests
Admin Should Never:
Invent or estimate pricing without authorization
Define scope, inclusions, or exclusions
Make warranty or outcome promises
Send unapproved service agreements
Commit to treatment specifics not in the system
📋
Documentation Rule
If a customer asks about pricing or scope and you're routing it, document the question and that you routed it. Don't leave the customer without a clear next step — "I'll have someone follow up with pricing details by [timeframe]" is far better than silence.
In Practice
Your role is to gather complete information, help move documents through the process, and make sure the customer receives accurate, approved materials. If the information is not already approved or documented, route it to the appropriate person.

Complaints & Callbacks

1
Acknowledge the concern
2
Collect the details
3
Check service history
4
Identify the issue type (see right)
5
Document the facts — not interpretations
6
Schedule, assign, or escalate
In Practice
A customer may say "the treatment didn't work" — but you need details. What are they seeing now? Where? When did service happen? Is it the same issue or a new one? Do not debate. Gather facts, check history, and route appropriately.
Step 4 — Identify Which Type of Issue:
Continued activity after treatment
New or unrelated pest issue
Access problem prevented full service
Expectation mismatch
Service quality concern
Unclear — needs technician review
Suggested Response
I understand this is frustrating. I'm going to document the details, check the service history, and make sure the right person is looped in to determine the next step.
📋
Documentation Rule
Document what the customer reported — not what you think happened. The technician and manager will need the facts as stated, not your interpretation. Stick to specifics: what, where, when, how often.

Technician Coordination

Technician Briefing
What goes in every service record
📍
Correct address + unit or common area
e.g. Maple Court, Bldg B, Unit 204
👤
Contact name and how to reach them
Resident name, manager, or board contact
🔑
Access details
Gate code, lockbox, parking, key instructions
🐛
Pest concern and observed locations
What was seen, where, and when it started
🐾
Pets or tenant restrictions
Dog in unit, children present, prep required
📷
Photos or customer notes attached
Referenced in account record
⚠️
Urgency or complaint context
Board president involved, tenant upset, etc.
Before finalizing — confirm the technician knows:
Where exactly to go (not just the street address)
What the pest concern is and where it's been seen
How to access the property or unit
Who to contact on arrival if needed
Any restrictions: pets, children, tenant prep, parking
Whether this is a new issue or a callback
What incomplete records create:
Technician arrives at wrong unit or building
Can't access property — wasted trip
Wrong service performed due to unclear details
Customer frustration and additional callbacks
Manager time spent on avoidable issues
In Practice
The technician experience depends entirely on admin documentation. Missing gate codes, wrong unit numbers, unclear contact names, or incomplete pest details create delays. Before finalizing, ask: "If I were the technician, would I have everything I need?"
