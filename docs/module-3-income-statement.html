Module 3

Income Statement basics

Learning Objective

By the end of this module, you will be able to explain what the Income Statement shows, identify basic income categories, and recognize which Income Statement questions should be escalated to finance.

What you will learn

Module focus

  • What an Income Statement is used for
  • How it differs from the Balance Sheet
  • How to explain income, expenses, and net income
  • How to respond when activity appears unusual
GIM standard

Explain activity clearly

The Income Statement is often where boards look to understand monthly activity. Keep explanations tied to the report period, line items, and Buildium totals.

Key idea

The Income Statement answers: “What income and expenses were recorded during this reporting period?”

Purpose

A period-based view of financial activity

Unlike the Balance Sheet, which shows position as of one date, the Income Statement shows activity across a defined period.

The Income Statement helps answer:

  • What income was recorded?
  • What expenses were recorded?
  • Did the association have net income or a net loss?
  • Which line items contributed to the result?

Sample report context

  • Association: Test Association
  • Report: Income Statement
  • Period: 4/1/2026 - 4/30/2026
  • Basis: Accrual Basis

Buildium tip

Use “for the period” language when explaining this report. The sample Income Statement is for April activity, not the association’s entire financial position.

Income

Where the recorded income came from

The sample Income Statement shows three income lines for April, with total income of $3,715.

Association Fee Income $3,200 — income from regular association charges recorded in Buildium during the period.
Late Fee Income $15 — late fee income recorded during the period.
Interest Income $500 — interest income recorded to the association.
Total Income $3,715 — the combined total of the income lines shown on the report.

How to say it

“For April, Buildium shows total income of $3,715. That total is made up of association fee income, late fee income, and interest income.”

Result

Understanding the bottom lines

The bottom of the Income Statement summarizes the result after income and expenses are applied.

Net Operating Income

Operating income minus operating expenses shown on the report. In the sample, this amount is $3,715.

Net Income

The final result after all income and expense categories shown on the report. In the sample, this amount is also $3,715.

Why the two numbers match here

In the sample Income Statement, no expenses are shown for April. Because there are no expenses or additional non-operating items on this report, Net Operating Income and Net Income are the same.

Use caution

Do not assume these two lines will always match. In a full operating month with expenses, non-operating activity, or other adjustments, the totals may differ.

Timing

No expenses does not automatically mean something is wrong

A board member may notice that the sample Income Statement shows income but no expenses for April. Keep the response factual and verify before assuming the reason.

No expenses entered There may simply be no expense activity recorded in Buildium for the selected period.
Timing difference Vendor bills or other expenses may be recorded in another month.
Not yet billed or received An expected cost may not have been invoiced or entered yet.
Accrual question Questions about whether something should have been accrued should be escalated to finance.

Recommended phrasing

“This Income Statement does not show expenses for April. I can verify whether there were no expenses entered for the period or whether there is a timing item finance should review.”

Board-facing explanation

Answer factual activity questions, escalate accounting judgment

Income Statement questions are usually safe for PMs when they involve identifying the period, reading the totals, and explaining line items in plain language.

PM can usually answer

What income was recorded? Where did the income come from? Was there net income or loss? Which period is shown? What line items appear?

Escalate to finance

Should this have been accrued? Why was this recognized in April? Is the income coded correctly? Are expenses missing? Does this need adjustment?

Recommended phrasing

“For April, Buildium shows $3,715 of income and no expenses on this Income Statement, resulting in net income of $3,715. I can confirm the accounting timing with finance if needed.”

Wrap-up

Income Statement key takeaways

Module key takeaways

  • The Income Statement shows financial activity for a defined reporting period.
  • It summarizes income, expenses, and the resulting net income or net loss.
  • The sample report shows total income of $3,715 and no expenses for April.
  • No expenses on the report does not automatically mean something is wrong.
  • Timing, accrual, revenue recognition, coding, and adjustment questions should be escalated to finance.

PM standard

Start with the period, the line item, and the amount. Keep the explanation grounded in what Buildium is showing.
