Module 5

Delinquent Association Owners report

Learning Objective

By the end of this module, you will be able to explain owner delinquency balances, read aging buckets, describe what charges make up the balance, and identify when collections or ledger questions should be escalated.

What you will learn

Module focus

  • What the Delinquent Association Owners report shows
  • How to read owner balances and delinquency status
  • How to explain aging buckets and charge breakdowns
  • How to discuss owner balances professionally
GIM standard

Be factual and careful

Delinquency information is operationally important and sensitive. Keep explanations neutral, use the Buildium report language, and avoid speculation about owner circumstances.

Key idea

This report answers: “Who has a past-due owner balance, how much is owed, and how old is the balance?”

Purpose

A point-in-time view of past-due owner balances

The Delinquent Association Owners report shows owner balances that are past due as of the report date. It is used to help the board understand delinquency exposure and collection follow-up needs.

The report helps answer:

  • Which owner accounts are delinquent?
  • How much is currently past due?
  • How old are the unpaid balances?
  • Is the account marked with a delinquency status?
  • What charge categories make up the balance?

Sample report context

  • Association: Test Association
  • Report: Delinquent Association Owners
  • Report date: As of 4/30/2026
  • Owner scope: Future/Current owners
  • System: Buildium-generated report

Buildium tip

Always check the “as of” date before answering. A payment received after that date may not be reflected in the report.

Owner Balance

Start with the owner line, status, and total

Sample owner line

  • Unit: 2
  • Owner: TEST OWNER2
  • Delinquency status: In collections
  • Last payment: None received
  • Total balance: $430

Plain-language explanation

“As of the report date, Unit 2 shows a delinquent balance of $430. Buildium lists the account as in collections and shows no payment received.”

Keep the explanation tied to what the report displays.

Escalation watch-out

The PM can read the status shown in Buildium. Questions about legal action, attorney activity, payment plan terms, or next collection steps should follow GIM’s escalation process.

Aged Balances

Aging buckets show how long the balance has been unpaid

Aging buckets do not just show the total delinquency. They show whether the balance is recent or has been outstanding for a longer period.

Sample aging breakdown

0–30 days: $215 31–60 days: $215 61–90 days: $0 91+ days: $0

Board-facing explanation

“The total delinquent balance is $430. Of that, $215 is 0–30 days old and $215 is 31–60 days old. There is no balance shown in the 61–90 or 91+ day buckets.”

Key idea

Aging helps the board distinguish between a newer past-due balance and a longer-running delinquency concern.

Charges

The report also shows what makes up the balance

Sample charge categories

  • Association Fee Income: $400
  • Late Fee Income: $30
  • Total delinquent balance: $430

How to explain it

“The balance is made up of $400 in association fees and $30 in late fees.”

This is helpful when the board asks whether the balance is regular assessments, late fees, fines, or another charge type.

Do not overstep

The PM can explain the charge categories shown. Questions about whether a charge should be waived, reversed, or disputed need the approved internal process and may require finance or collections review.

Communication Standard

Discuss delinquency information neutrally and only with authorized parties

Use this approach

Do

  • Use neutral, factual language
  • Refer to the Buildium report date
  • Discuss owner details only with authorized parties
  • Separate report facts from collection decisions
  • Escalate disputes or legal questions
Avoid this approach

Do not

  • Speculate about why the owner has not paid
  • Use judgmental language
  • Share owner balance details casually
  • Promise a collection outcome
  • Approve waivers outside process

Better wording

Say, “The report shows a delinquent balance,” not “the owner refuses to pay.” Say, “Buildium shows no payment received,” not “they never pay.”

Wrap-Up

What the PM can answer and when to escalate

PM can answer

Factual report questions

  • How much is delinquent?
  • Which aging bucket is the balance in?
  • Is the account marked in collections?
  • What charges make up the balance?
  • What does the report show as of the report date?
Escalate

Process, legal, or ledger questions

  • What legal action has been taken?
  • Can the association waive late fees?
  • Is the owner disputing the ledger?
  • Should a charge be reversed?
  • What collection step comes next?

Module takeaway

The Delinquent Association Owners report is practical and sensitive. Use it to answer basic factual questions, protect owner confidentiality, and escalate collection, legal, waiver, or ledger disputes.
