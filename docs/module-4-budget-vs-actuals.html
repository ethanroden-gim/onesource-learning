Module 4

Budget vs. Actuals basics

Learning Objective

By the end of this module, you will be able to interpret actual, budget, variance, and percent-of-budget columns and explain basic budget performance to a board.

What you will learn

Module focus

  • What Budget vs. Actuals is used for
  • How to read monthly, year-to-date, and annual budget columns
  • How to explain variance and percent of budget
  • When a variance is operational context versus a finance escalation
GIM standard

Explain performance with context

This report helps boards understand whether financial activity is tracking to the approved budget. Keep explanations tied to the timeframe, line item, and Buildium totals.

Key idea

Budget vs. Actuals answers: “How did recorded activity compare to what we expected in the budget?”

Purpose

A comparison between actual activity and the approved budget

The Budget vs. Actuals report gives the board context. It does not just show what happened; it shows how actual activity compares to the budgeted expectation.

The report helps answer:

  • Are we over or under budget?
  • Which line items are driving the difference?
  • Is the difference monthly, year-to-date, or annual?
  • Does the variance look timing-related or operationally important?

Sample report context

  • Association: Test Association
  • Report: Budget vs. Actuals
  • Monthly section: 4/1/2026 - 4/30/2026
  • Year-to-date section: 1/1/2026 - 4/30/2026
  • Basis: Accrual Basis

Buildium tip

Always confirm whether the board is asking about the current month, year-to-date performance, or the full annual budget before giving an answer.

Columns

How to read the core Budget vs. Actuals columns

Most board questions can be answered by reading these five column types correctly.

Actual What was recorded in Buildium for the selected period.
Budget What the approved budget expected for that same period.
Variance The difference between actual and budget. Context depends on income versus expense.
% of Budget How much of the budget has been achieved or used for the period shown.
Annual Budget The full-year approved budget amount for the line item.

How to say it

“Actual is what Buildium shows happened. Budget is what was expected. Variance is the difference between those two numbers.”

Timeframe

Read monthly and year-to-date separately

A line item can look favorable for the month and still be behind year-to-date, or the reverse. Do not blend the sections together.

Monthly section

Shows April activity compared to April budget. In the sample report, this is the 4/1/2026 - 4/30/2026 section.

Year-to-date section

Shows activity from January through April compared to the budget for the same year-to-date period.

Common issue

A board member may ask, “Are we over budget?” The better response is to clarify: “Are you looking at April only, or year-to-date?”

PM standard

State the timeframe every time: “For April...” or “Year-to-date through April...”

Income

Association Fee Income shows why timeframe matters

In the sample report, Association Fee Income is above budget for April but below budget year-to-date.

April Association Fee Income

  • Actual: $3,200
  • Budget: $1,000
  • Variance: $2,200
  • % of Budget: 320%

Year-to-date Association Fee Income

  • Actual: $3,400
  • Budget: $4,000
  • Variance: ($600)
  • % of Budget: 85%

Recommended phrasing

“April Association Fee Income is above the monthly budget, but year-to-date it is still $600 below budget.”

Expenses

Low spending may be favorable or timing-related

Maintenance & Repairs shows no actual spending through April, even though the budget expected some spending by this point.

April Maintenance & Repairs

  • Actual: $0
  • Budget: $166.67
  • Variance: ($166.67)
  • % of Budget: 0%

Year-to-date Maintenance & Repairs

  • Actual: $0
  • Budget: $666.67
  • Variance: ($666.67)
  • Annual Budget: $2,000

Use caution

Do not automatically call lower expense activity “savings.” The work may simply not have happened yet, or the invoice may not have been entered.

Recommended phrasing

“Maintenance & Repairs has no actual spending recorded through April compared to a year-to-date budget of $666.67. This may be a timing item unless we know the work will not occur.”

Annual budget

Some budgeted costs may not appear evenly each month

Some expenses are annual, seasonal, or event-driven. The Budget vs. Actuals report may show an annual budget even when monthly or year-to-date budget activity is zero.

Insurance Annual Budget: $8,000. April actual, April budget, year-to-date actual, and year-to-date budget all show $0.
Interest Expense Annual Budget: $1,000. April budget is $83.33, with no actual expense recorded.
Total Expenses April actual is $0 against a monthly budget of $250. Year-to-date actual is $0 against a budget of $1,000.
Timing matters Budget timing can depend on how the budget was entered and when costs are expected to occur.

How to say it

“Insurance has an annual budget of $8,000, but this report does not show monthly or year-to-date budget activity for that line through April.”

Variance

Favorable or unfavorable depends on the line item

Variance is not automatically good or bad. The meaning changes depending on whether the line is income or expense and whether timing is involved.

Income variance

Higher income than budget is usually favorable Lower income than budget is usually unfavorable or timing-related Confirm whether collections, billing, or timing explain the difference

Expense variance

Lower expense than budget may be favorable Lower expense may also mean delayed work or invoices Higher expense may require operational or accounting explanation

Do not overstate

Instead of saying, “We saved money,” say, “Spending is below budget through April; we should confirm whether that is true savings or timing.”

Explain

Summarize the result, then identify the drivers

A useful Budget vs. Actuals explanation includes the timeframe, the net result, and the main line items driving the variance.

Sample April summary

For April, net income is $3,715 compared to a monthly budget of $766.67, resulting in a positive variance of $2,948.33.

Sample year-to-date summary

Year-to-date net income is $3,930 compared to a budget of $3,066.67, resulting in a positive variance of $863.33.

Recommended phrasing

“The main drivers are higher April association fee income and interest income, while no expenses are recorded through April. We should treat some expense variances as timing items unless confirmed otherwise.”

Escalate when needed

Reforecasting, budget coding disputes, accrual questions, and requests to adjust financial activity should be escalated to finance.

Wrap-up

Budget vs. Actuals key takeaways

Module key takeaways

  • Budget vs. Actuals compares recorded activity to the approved budget.
  • Always separate monthly results from year-to-date results.
  • Actual is what Buildium shows happened; budget is what was expected.
  • Variance needs context, especially for expenses that may be timing-related.
  • Reforecasting, accruals, coding, and correction questions should be escalated to finance.

PM standard

State the timeframe, read the actual and budget columns, explain the variance, and avoid calling timing differences “savings” until confirmed.
