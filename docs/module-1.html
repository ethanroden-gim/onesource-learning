Module 1

What this financial package is — and the PM’s role

Learning Objective

By the end of this module, you will be able to explain the purpose of a Buildium-generated financial package and identify the property manager’s role in basic financial conversations.

What you will learn

Module focus

  • What a financial package is used for
  • How the reports work together
  • What a property manager should explain
  • When to involve finance
GIM standard

Clear. Accurate. Transparent.

Property managers are not expected to replace finance. They are expected to help boards understand the basics, stay factual, and avoid guessing.

Key idea

A confident PM can say: “I can explain what this Buildium report is showing, and I’ll have finance confirm the accounting detail.”

Purpose

A monthly snapshot of financial activity

A financial package gives the board a structured way to review what happened, what is outstanding, and where more detail may be needed.

It helps the board understand:

  • What the association owns
  • What the association owes
  • What income and expenses were recorded
  • How actual results compare to budget
  • Which owners are delinquent
  • What transactions support the totals

The sample package includes:

  • Balance Sheet
  • Income Statement
  • Budget vs. Actuals
  • Delinquent Association Owners
  • Transaction Detail by Account
Role clarity

Explain the report. Do not guess.

The property manager’s role is to guide the board through the financial package. PMs must stick to reporting factual data and avoid speculating on how transactions are recorded or making unsupported financial conclusions.

Explain

Identify the report, read the relevant section, define basic terms, and point to the total or line item that answers the question.

Do not guess

Do not speculate about journal entries, accruals, reclassifications, prior-period activity, or whether an accounting entry is correct.

PMs should be able to:

  • Identify the correct report
  • Explain what the report shows
  • Point to the relevant line item or total
  • Answer simple factual questions
  • Escalate accounting interpretation to finance

Use report-based language:

  • “The report is showing…”
  • “As of the report date…”
  • “For this reporting period…”
  • “Finance should confirm the accounting treatment.”
Buildium basics

Read the report labels before answering

Because these reports are generated from Buildium, the date, period, basis, and generated timestamp all affect how you explain the report.

Association Name Confirms you are reviewing the correct property or association.
Report Title Tells you which type of question the report can answer.
Date or Period Shows whether the report is point-in-time or activity over a period.
Accounting Basis May show accrual basis, cash basis, or another reporting basis.
Generated Timestamp Helps determine whether recent transactions may be included.
Page Count Confirms whether you have the full report.

Buildium tip

If someone asks whether a recent payment, invoice, or adjustment is included, check the report’s generated date and time before answering.

Escalation boundary

Reading the report vs. interpreting accounting treatment

A useful rule: if the question is about reading the report, the PM can usually answer. If the question is about accounting treatment, correction, classification, or whether something should have been booked differently, finance should answer.

PM can usually answer

Which report shows cash? How much income was recorded? Which owner balances are delinquent? What line item is over budget? What transactions make up this total?

Escalate to finance

Was this journal entry booked correctly? Why is equity negative? Should this be accrued? Should this expense be reclassified? Does this require a correction?
Wrap-up

The PM standard

Module key takeaways

  • Buildium financial packages combine summary reports and detail reports.
  • Property managers should explain what the reports show.
  • Property managers should not guess on accounting treatment.
  • Escalation protects accuracy, transparency, and board trust.

Use this sentence often

“I can explain what Buildium is showing here. Finance should confirm the accounting detail behind that entry.”
